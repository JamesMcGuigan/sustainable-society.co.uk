Feasta/Barataria Network Conference

May 19th and 20th.
The Central Hotel. 1-5 Exchequer Street, Dublin 2 Interest payments are one of the reasons why national economies have to grow continually to avoid collapse. They also transfer cash from the poor to the rich. So can we set up savings and loan systems which don't charge it?
To settle this question, Feasta in partnership with the Baratana Network (the Barataria Foundation of Scotland, Barataria Nederland and Barataria Espana) has called a one-day conference on interest-free banking for Saturday, May 19th. The morning session will discuss interest generally and in particular the difference would it make to the economy and society if money was spent into circulation by government rather than being lent into circulation by the banks. Bernard Lietaer, James Robertson, Michael Rowbotham and Richard Douthwaite will speak.
In the afternoon, discussions will focus on the advantages of interest-free banks and the practicalities of setting them up. Speakers will include Oscar Kjellberg, the chairman of the interest-free JAK bank in Sweden, Pat Conaty, of the Aston Reinvestment Trust in Birmingham, which is planning interest-free loans for house repairs, and Naomi Kingsley of the London Rebuilding Society, which is about to launch a mutual aid fund to provide interest-free loans to small businesses.
Many participants will be staying on in Dublin on the Sunday after the conference ends for private discussions. One topic will be Feasts's project for an interest-free bank in Dublin. For detailed programme e-mail Marc van der Valk at Barataria Netherlands
valk@barataria.org
