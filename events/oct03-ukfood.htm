The Future of Food and Agriculture after Cancun


An Invitation to the UK Food Group Seminars, 16, 25 September – 16, 23 October 2003


The UK Food Group is the leading UK network for non-governmental organisations (NGO’s) working on global food and agriculture issues. The UK Food Group represents 32 development, farming, consumer and environment organisations, drawn together by a common concern for food security.

This autumn we are hosting a series of seminars on current trends in global food trade and its impact on agriculture and food systems. The seminar on the 16th of October, World Food Day, will focus on the outcomes of the 5th WTO Ministerial in Cancun and explore future options for food security and food sovereignty.

Each afternoon seminar will begin with a buffet lunch from 1pm. Presentations will commence at 2pm, followed by discussion, finishing at 4:30. To book a place, please RSVP to the UK Food Group on the contact details below.

Pesticide dependency in Africa: Changing agriculture to support rural livelihoods Tuesday 16th September 2 – 4:30pm Venue: TUC Congress House, 28 Great Russell Street, London. Nearest underground: Tottenham Ct Road This seminar will present the findings of Pesticide Action Network’s research involving over 400 smallholder farmers in Africa. The study documents the alarming rate of pesticide dependency, the unintended side effects and often the failure to solve the pest problems. How are current pesticide use and trading practices causing problems for production costs, debt, health and environmental harm? What strategies are needed to re-orientate practices to reduce pesticide reliance?Speakers: Stephanie Williamson, PAN UK; Seth Gogoe, adviser on ethical trade in agriculture, Ghana; Dr Janny Vos, CABI Bioscience UK Centre, Department for International Development.

Agricultural technologies and sustainable livelihoods: What relevance do GM crops have for poor farmers? Thursday 25th September 2 – 4:30pm Venue: TUC Congress House, 28 Great Russell Street, London. Nearest underground: Tottenham Ct RoadWhat are developing country small-scale farmers’ experiences of growing GM so far? How can they participate in setting the priorities for agricultural R&D? What problems can be caused by trade in GM crops and GM food aid? What sustainable farming methods are already in common use and how can these be mainstreamed?Speakers: Martin Kimani, CABl Africa Regional Centre, Kenya; Stella Semino, Grupo de Reflexion Rural, Argentina; Clare Devereux, UK GM Public Debate Steering Board; Alex Wijeratna, Action Aid; Institute of Development Studies.

WORLD FOOD DAY - Governing agriculture: What now for food security and food sovereignty, after Cancun? Thursday 16th October 2 – 4:30pm Venue: Partnership House, 157 Waterloo Road, London. Nearest underground: Waterloo Has Cancun delivered on the Doha Development Agenda? What progress was made on the Agreement on Agriculture and the Patents and Food negotiations in TRIPs? What will this mean for the future of small-scale agriculture? What governance mechanisms, including the UN, are needed at different levels to ensure food security and food sovereignty in developing countries?Speakers include: Michael Meacher MP, former Environment Minister; PV Satheesh, Deccan Development Society, India

Trans-national Corporations: The chains of agriculture? Thursday 23rd October 2 – 4:30pm Venue: Partnership House, 157 Waterloo Road, London. Nearest underground: WaterlooThis will be an opportunity to discuss the findings of new research by the UK Food Group on buyer power in the food chain from the farm to the consumer. How does the buyer power of traders, processors and retailers affect farmers, workers and the environment in both developed and developing countries? What policy interventions are available for redressing the negative impacts?Speakers include: Bill Vorley, research author, International Institute for Environment and Development (IIED)
