Rally for a Sustainable Britain

A day conference
Saturday April 6th
Westminster Central Hall
London Speakers include: Rt Hon Robin Cook MP, Charles Secrett, Bianca Jagger, Sara Parkin, Madeline Bunting, Peter Melchett (tbc) and more..... While Labour's first term in office delivered some moves towards sustainability, there is a long way to go. SERA believes that Labour's second term must be used to deliver a positive vision of a sustainable Britain.
A sustainable Britain will require a far more fundamental shift towards industries such as renewable energy, waste recycling and low input agriculture. In a sustainable Britain, certain aspects of our lifestyle will have to change e.g. our attitude to the car, as well as the way we dispose of waste and consume energy.
Above all, a sustainable Britain needs political leadership. The benefits for Britain of a sustainable economy and society are well documented and, SERA believes, within our reach.
This conference will give delegates the opportunity to demonstrate their commitment to the idea of a sustainable Britain. Throughout the day, senior politicians and environmental commentators will outline their visions of sustainability and delegates will have the opportunity to question and challenge the speakers. Workshops on 8 different topics will explore:
  • How can the government deliver a sustainability programme in this area?
  • How can we, as individuals, act sustainably and get others to do so?
Delegates will be encouraged to 'pledge' to change at least one aspect of their own life-style, as a result of the day. And finally, SERA will forward the key messages from the conference, to the government.
