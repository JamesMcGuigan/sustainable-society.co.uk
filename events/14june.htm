THE FIRST LONDON SCHUMACHER LECTURES

On 14th June 2001 the Schumacher Society and UNED Forum are holding the first annual London Schumacher Lectures. To be held at the Banqueting House in Whitehall from 2.3Opm to 730pm. This is a high- profile part of the UK Preparations for the World Summit on Sustainable Development held in Johannesburg, September 2002.

'GOVERNANCE, BUSINESS AND SUSTAINABLE DEVELOPMENT'

THE SPEAKERS

Nitin Desai, is United Nations Under Secretary-General for Policy Co-ordination and Sustainable Development. He is Director-General of the UN World Summit on Sustainable Development, Johannesburg, September 2002. Previously he was Secretary and Chief Economic Adviser in India's Ministry of Finance.
Sir Mark Moody-Stuart, is currently chairman of Royal Dutch/Shell and from July 2001 will lead the Business Action for Sustainable Development, a joint initiative by the World Business Council for Sustainable Development & the International Chamber of Commerce. In 2000 he became co-chair of the G8 Renewable Energy Taskforce. In 1997 he launched Shell Renewables.
Ann Pettifor, Pettifor is director of the Jubilee Plus at the New Economics Foundation, London. She was founder and director of Jubilee 2000, which succeeded in having $110 billion debt cancelled for the poorest nations of the world. Jubilee Plus provides research and analysis to debt campaigns around the world.
John Elkington, is chairman of SustainAbility, London, a leading consultancy on business strategies for sustainable development. He works with leading companies, with governments and NGOs. He has (co)-authored 15 books, including Cannibals with Forks:The Triple Bottom Line of 21st Century Business.
Barry Coates, is director of the World Development Movement, London. Before that he was Head of Development Policy at the World Wide Fund for Nature (WWF-UK). He has worked extensively in developing countries, mainly in the Pacific and Africa. He also advised unions and industry for a US think tank.
In addition to these speakers, there will be one or two surprise guests to contribute to the event.

THE LECTURES

The first London Lectures will explore how improved information flow between government, business, and civil-society can enhance the implementation of sustainable development. Earth Summit 2002 will, above all else, seek to define new ways of producing tangible solutions - nationally and internationally - to the key environmental problems of our time.
  • Global warming.
  • The loss of biodiversity.
  • A decline in environmental security.
All these topics present stakeholders with problems that they cannot solve alone. How then can we ensure that politicians, business leaders, and the public see sustainability as central to our way of life and the implementation of future social objectives?
The focus of the Lectures is the UN Summit on Sustainable Development in Johannesburg in September 2002. The Lectures and the question-and-answer sessions will explore what government and business is doing to implement sustainable development.
  • Can available knowledge on this be used more effectively in national governance, and in the management of companies and public bodies?
  • How can improved information flows between politicians, business and society at large enhance the implementation of sustainable development?
  • How can new perspectives on governance and management contribute to world-wide sustainability?
The London Schumacher Lectures will allow an audience of 400 people to listen to and enter a debate with six or more high level speakers in an afternoon and early evening event. We want to explore how we can bring the theme of 'sustainability' centre stage into decision making by politicians, business leaders, opinion formers and the general public.

THE SCHUMACHER LECTURES

Since 1979 Schumacher Lectures have been held at major venues in Bristol. They have been dubbed 'the UK's premier environmental gathering' by the Guardian. Since 1996 Schumacher Lectures have also been held in Liverpool and Manchester, and since 2001 in Edinburgh and Dublin. From 2001 the annual London Schumacher Lectures will augment the programme of conferences and events organised by UNED Forum and the Schumacher Society in this joint initiative in London.

THE DAY

The event will consist of two afternoon sessions, 2:30 to 4:30 and 5:30 to 7:30 PM. Each session will include short lectures followed by a period of questions and answers. It is expected that an audience of 400 will include key representatives from business, politics, academia, the media, and NGQs. The price of admission will be such to encourage the public to attend.
