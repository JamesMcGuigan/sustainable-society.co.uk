Governance of multinationals is acquainted with the idea of 'foreign direct liability'. The question whether multinationals should be sued in developed countries for their unsustainable practices in the emerging ones that do not possess a good environmental liability system is very acute in times of industrial globalisation.
Many actors have an interest in responding to the possibility of successful transnational environment and health and safety litigation; from governments and multinationals to the public. On the other side, many actors would want this topic left on the shelves of academia.

For those concerned with environmental protection per se, a crucial question is whether support for transnational litigation also means environmental protection. Does the prospect of a better managed system really encourage sustainable development in the field or is it simply a new layer of bureaucracy to an overbooked international system?
